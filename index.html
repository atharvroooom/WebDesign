Mr. Christopher Hitchens

The God of modern-day atheism

Here's a time line of Mr. Hitchens' life:

  • 1949 - Born in Portsmouth, Hampshire to a Anglo-Saxon family.
  • 1967 - Leaves his home to attend the Ballilol College at Oxford where he read Philosophy, Politics, and Economics.
  • 1970 - Graduated from college with a third class degree.
  • 1971 - After spending a year travelling the United States on a scholarship, Hitchens went to work at the Times Higher Education Supplement where he served as a social science correspondent.
  • 1989 - Hitchens met Carol Blue in Los Angeles. They married in 1991.
  • 1992 - Hitchens became a contributing editor of Vanity Fair.
  • 2007 - Christopher became an American Citizen.
  • 2005 - he was named as fifth on the list of the "Top 100 Public Intellectuals" by Foreign Policy and Prospect magazines.
  • 2007 - Hitchens congregates with Richard Dawkins, Sam Harris, and Daniel Dennett at his residence for a private, unmoderated discussion lasting two hours. The event was videotaped and entitled "The Four Horsemen".
  • 2007 - Hitchens published his magnum-opus, 'God is not Great'.
  • 2007-2011 - Hitchens championed the New Age Atheism movement and did a plethora of debates with notable religious figures over the world on Theism and its ramifications on the human condition and the world as a whole.
  • 2010 - He was diagnosed with Oesophagal cancer.
  • 2011 - Hitchens died of pneumonia on 15 December, in the University of Texas MD Anderson Cancer Center, Houston, aged 62. In accordance with his wishes, his body was donated to medical research.

An astonishingly articulate and an erudite personality; in the age old war between Jerusalem and Athens, Hitchens was a champion for the latter. Unrelenting in his defense of the rational and poetic, he spent most of his life fighting for free speech and freedom of inquiry. Undoubtedly so, he managed to raise awareness in the global concsious against dogmatism and fanaticism. Bereft of words to end this tribute to do justice to his work, I leave with a quote of his own- quite a condensation of his core values as an individual.


My own opinion is enough for me, and I claim the right to have it defended against any consensus, any majority, anywhere, anyplace, anytime. And anyone who disagrees with this can pick a number, get in line, and kiss my arse.

-- Mr. Christopher Hitchens
